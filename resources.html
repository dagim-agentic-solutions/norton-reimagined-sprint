4 items
  • 01

    ArticleThreat Actor Abuse of AI Accelerates from Tool to Cyberattack Surface

    Microsoft Security Blog · April 2026 — Fresh from RSAC 2026, Microsoft frames the shift from generative to agentic threat models — attackers embedding AI across the full attack lifecycle and the agent ecosystem itself becoming the most-attacked surface. Sets up the "why now" for repositioning Norton beyond reactive antivirus.

  • 02

    ArticleBest Identity Theft Protection Services 2026

    idpicks.com · 2026 — Side-by-side breakdown of LifeLock, Aura, Bitdefender, and Identity Guard on the identity-plus-cyber bundle dimension — the exact competitive set Norton Reimagined is repositioning against. Surfaces Norton's renewal-pricing tension, useful when pressure-testing the tiering model.

  • 03

    VideoHow to Design for Human-Agent Interaction

    Karri Saarinen, CEO of Linear · Every · Feb 2026 — Linear's six-principle framework for how agents and humans should share a product surface, plus the accountability question: who's responsible when an agent sends the email you meant to review? The closest prior art to the three-tab architecture and five-level agentic authority model.

  • 04

    ArticleThe Fiduciary in the Machine

    Zwillgen · Jan 2026 — Articulates the "AI-as-tool vs. AI-as-agent" distinction and the supervision paradox that emerges when one human nominally oversees thousands of AI-managed accounts. Gives the sprint team precise language for why Norton's fiduciary-model Digital Safety Advisor is a defensible strategic stance, not marketing veneer.
